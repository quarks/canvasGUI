Web-safe fonts are fonts widely supported across various 
operating systems and browsers. Important for consistent 
and reliable display, they make sure a website appears as 
intended on different devices. Common examples include 
Arial, Helvetica and Times New Roman, minimizing the risk 
of font rendering issues, such as slower website load times 
and enhancing the site's accessibility and usability.
<pl>
Like all other types of fonts, web-safe fonts are categorized 
according to their generic font families. A generic font 
family is a category or classification of fonts that share 
common characteristics or design elements. There are five 
generic font families: serif, sans-serif, monospace, cursive 
and fantasy.
